Knowledge of the relationship between the gas and dust
components is essential to understanding the star formation
process because of the possibility that at high densities the gas
molecules are indirectly heated by collisions with the warm
dust grains (Goidreich & Kwan 1974). The energy transfer
between the dust and gas components which would be sufficient to keep the gas temperature close to the dust temperature
requires n(H2) > iO~ cm ~, and gas-dust coupling is expected
at n(H2) > iO'~ cm ~ (Solomon 1978). Our results for NGC

The gas-dust coupling is a complicated problem. It is well
understood that there are multiple components of the dust
(e.g., Crawford & Rowan-Robinson 1986; de Jong & Brink
1987; Helou 1987; Cox & Mezger 1987; Xu & De Zotti 1989;
Devereux & Young 1990, 1992). Some papers have tried to
determine the normalized distribution of dust grains as a function of temperature using IRAS and FIR/submillimeter continuum data (e.g., Pajot et al. 1986; Heyer et al. 1989; Xie,
Goldsmith, & Zhou 1991 ; Kwan & Xie 1992). Warm and cool
components of gas have also been discussed with respect to
several galaxies (e.g., Eckart et al. 1990; Mauersberger, Henkel,
& Sage 1990; Mauersberger et al. 1991 ; Harris et al. 1991). The
global dust temperature calculated from the IRAS data mdicates the presence of warm dust, while the average gas temperature from `2C0 and `3C0 data is derived by simply
assuming a single gas component. The coupling between the
various components of the gas and the dust cannot be sufficiently understood in the present work. More high-resolution
infrared continuum and molecular line observations on the
same scale are necessary to understand the coupling between
the gas and dust components over more extended regions in
galaxies.

7. SUMMARY

The nuclear regions of NGC 2146 and IC 342 have been
observed in `2C0 and `3C0 J = 2-1 and 1-0, as well as CS
J = 2-1 emission with the FCRAO 14 m telescope. The `2C0
J = 2-1 and 1-0 line shapes in the central 45" of these actively
star-forming galaxies are remarkably similar.
