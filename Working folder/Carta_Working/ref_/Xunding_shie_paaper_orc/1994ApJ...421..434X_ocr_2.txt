2. OBSERVATIONS

The observations were carried out primarily during the
periods of 1988 January-March and 1989 January-March and
December, using the Five College Radio Astronomy Observatory 14 m radome enclosed telescope, except for the 12C0
J = 1-0 observations for NGC 2146 which were accomplished
in 1987 as part of the FCRAO Extragalactic CO Survey. In
order to avoid the calibration and pointing uncertainties
associated with daytime heating of the antenna, the data were
only acquired under night conditions. All 12C0 and ~ 3C0
spectra were taken using a double beam-switching mode with a
spacing of 12~6 between the main and reference beams in the
azimuthal direction and a chopping frequency of 15 Hz. This
procedure canceled both sky and radiometer drifts and assured
that flat baselines were achieved. The rms pointing accuracy of
,~ 5" at the FCRAO 14 m telescope is based on pointing measurements of SiO masers and planets. Daily pointing for the
data presented here was checked on IRC + 10216 or Jupiter,
and confirmed by monitoring the line profile of the central
position ofeach galaxy.
The resolution (HPBW) of the 14 m antenna at the 12C0
J = 1-0 frequency (115.2712 GHz) and J = 2-1 frequency
(230.5379 GHz) is 45" and 23", respectively. At the distance of
IC 342 (4.5 Mpc), the linear sizes spanned by the two beams are
1.0 and 0.5 kpc, and at the distance of NGC 2146 (20.6 Mpc),
the corresponding sizes are 4.5 and 2.2 kpc. The receiver
system employed a cooled Schottky barrier diode mixer and a
quasi-optical single sideband filter with cold image termination. The typical system temperatures (SSB) were 800 K for the

J = 2-1 transitions and 500 K for the J = 1-0 transitions at
600 elevation. During the last week of 1989, the sky was exceptionally clear, and the outside temperature was well below zero
(around - 200 C), so the system temperature for the CS
J = 2-1 observations was only about 300 K. Velocity
resolution was provided by a 512 x 1 MHz filterbank, yielding
a total bandwidth of 1300 km s ` in 2.6 km s ` channels for
the CO J - 1-0 lines, and of a bandwidth of 650 km ~ in 1.3
km ` channels for the CO J = 2-1 lines. The velocity
resolution of all the final spectra presented in this paper has
been smoothed to 15.6 km ` in NGC 2146 and 7.8 km ` in
IC 342.
