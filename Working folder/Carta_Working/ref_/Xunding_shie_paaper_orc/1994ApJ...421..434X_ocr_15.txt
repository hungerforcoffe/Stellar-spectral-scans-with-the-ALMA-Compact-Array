6. COMPARISONS AMONG GALAXIES

6.1. Gas and Dust Coupling

Although the `2C0 and `3C0 f = 2-1 observations in galaxies are still relatively scarce compared with the available
extragalactic `2C0 f = 1-0 data (see Young et al. 1989), a fair
amount of extragalactic ~ 3CO f = 1-0 data have been
published (e.g., Rickard & Blitz 1985; Young & Sanders 1986;
Sage & Isbell 1991 ; Aalto et al. 1991). The simple model results
obtained here may be used to understand the observational
data in other galaxies. This in turn permits the NGC 2146 and
IC 342 data to be placed in a broader context.
Based on the radiative transfer calculations and the
observed data, we look into the dependence of the ` 2CO/' 3C0
f = 1-0 ratio on the average dust temperature, and the possibility that such a dependence can approximately be seen as a
dependence on gas temperature. In principle, if the ` 2C0 emission is optically thick and the ` 3C0 emission is optically thin,
the expected `2CO/13C0 line ratio is roughly proportional to
the gas temperature because of the dependence of opacity on
excitation temperature. The ratio seems to be also related
to the average dust temperature derived from IRAS

noticed that the global `2C0/' 3C0 f = 1-0 line ratio
