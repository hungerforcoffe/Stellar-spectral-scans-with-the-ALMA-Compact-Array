4.1.2. IC 342

At the center of IC 342, the 12C0 emission might come from
a partially optically thin gas. The ratio of `2C0 J = 2-1 and
1-0 emission is 1.1 ± 0.3 from our data. It is in good agreement
with Eckart et al. (1990), who also find a ratio of 1.1 ± 0.1 at
the center position of IC 342, although the coordinates differ
slightly from ours. An alternative explanation is that the emission could originate from a warm, optically thick gas with the
J = 2-1 line sampling a hotter component (Eckart et al. 1990).
Unlike NGC 2146, the ratio of `3C0 J = 2-1/f = 1-0 is low
in IC 342. It is only about 1.4 ± 0.4 at the center, which is far
lower than the expected value of 4 for a warm and optically

thin gas. Eckart et al. (1990) obtained a similarly lower ratio of
1.0 ± 0. 1 at the center of IC 342. The estimated excitation
temperature in the LTE approach is `~ 15 K, assuming the
1 ~ emission lines are optically thin.

4.2. 12C0/13C0 Ratio

is that the optical depth of the 12C0 emission can be estimated
from the `2CO/' 3C0 intensity ratio by taking the LTE
approach and assuming that both lines originates in the same
region, with only the relative calibration of the two lines
needed.
